JIEPU JIANG · UW-MADISON

My research helps people find, understand, and create text information. I apply a combination of information retrieval, data/text mining, and HCI methods to study search engines, conversational agents, writing assistants, and other text information systems.

Dr. Jiepu Jiang is an Assistant Professor in the Information School of the University of Wisconsin-Madison, which is a part of the School of Computer, Data & Information Sciences (CDIS). Before joining UW-Madison, he was an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Virginia Tech from 2018 to 2020. His research interests lie at the intersection of information retrieval and HCI, especially studying human-AI interaction issues in various text information systems. He holds bachelor's and master's degrees from Wuhan University, a Ph.D. in Library & Information Science from the University of Pittsburgh, and is finishing another Ph.D. in Computer Science at the University of Massachusetts Amherst.

Research

Conversational Agents & Chatbots

I study conversational agents, both text-based and voice-activated ones. My recent research has focused on human-AI hybrid systems supporting laypeople to finish knowledge-demanding conversations in a human-computation and crowdsourcing setting.

Related Articles: CIKM'21, SIGIR'20, CHIIR'16, WWW'15, ...

Explainability of Search Interfaces

I study human factors and interfaces for improving search system transparency and explainability, e.g., how search result summary's explainability affects click and other behavior, and how to improve search result summaries to better explain relevance and non-relevance.

Related Articles: SIGIR'21, SIGIR'19, ...


Search Quality & User Experience

I study methods for evaluating search quality and search user experience, e.g., adaptive effectiveness measures correlating with user experience in Cranfield-style evaluation and predictive models for search satisfaction based on behavioral signals.

Related Articles: SIGIR'17, CIKM'17, CHIIR'16, ECIR'16, WSDM'15, ...

Interactive Information Retrieval

I study human factors and behavior in search systems, including topics such as search task, search session, query reformulation, click decision, eye and mouse movement, relevance judgments, and intelligent search user interfaces.

Related Articles: CHIIR'17, CHIIR'16, WSDM'16, SIGIR'14, SIGIR'13, ...
